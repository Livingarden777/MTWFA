Php 3:18  For many walk, of whom I have told you often, and now tell you even weeping, that they are the enemies of the cross of Christ: 
Php 3:19  Whose end is destruction, whose God is their belly, and whose glory is in their shame, who mind earthly things.

Can you see from this verse how those who reject the Cross become carnal, earthly minded, and end up in the destruction of death?

This is exactly what happened to Adam and Eve when they tried to short-cut the process to Likeness. Their cross was to resist the temptation to eat of the wrong Tree, and to choose to eat of the Tree of Life. We endure the same test today, cloaked in a different form, but the same, nevertheless.

Summary: The purpose of life is God-likeness. Some will say those are the blasphemous words of the serpent, but really, the temptation from the devil was to take a shortcut to becoming like God. It is obvious from Gen 1:26 that God wants us to become like Him. Yahweh and Yashua also both bear witness to that later on in Scripture. In Psalm 82:6, we are called gods. In John 10:34, Yashua again calls us gods. Psalm 82:6 is not in reference to angels because angels are not “gods” by nature . Psalm 82:6 and John 10:34 are verses that speak to our identity as sons of Adam.

The devil will tempt you to pursue what you are called to be, without God or the cross. The real test in the garden was whether Adam and Eve would endure the cross to grow into God’s likeness. In rejecting the cross, humanity became carnal and ended up in the destruction of death. Today, we face different forms of the same test as Adam and Eve: we must resist the temptation to eat of the wrong Tree.
